How did the Financial Crisis create WW2?

Reparations imposed on Germany following WWI left the country poorer and economic woes caused resentment amongst its population. The Great Depression of the 1930s and a collapse in international trade also worsened Europe's financial situation, allowing Adolf Hitler and Joseph Stalin to rise to power on the promise of revitalization.

The Financial Crisis, worldwide economic downturn that began in 1929 ans lasted intill about 1939, caused drastic declines in output, severe unemployment, and acute deflation in almost every country of the world.
For many that survived the horrors of WWI, worse disruptions were in store. Inflation wreaked havoc with people's lives. Prices had doubled in Great Britain, The U.S and Japan. But nothing compared with the Hyperinflation ocurring in Germany, Asutria, Poland and Russia.
The Financial Crisis of the 1920s gave birth to Dictators all around Europe. For example, As a consequence of the Treaty of Versailles (After WWI) and the Financial crisis, Adolf Hittler and the Nazi Ideology was imposed in Germany. In the 1920, The Soviet States, also faced with solving economic problems, created their own dictator, Joseph Stalin.
